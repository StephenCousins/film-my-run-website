# 1066 Ultramarathon 2019 - Research

**Stephen's Race:** 07/07/2019 | 26:46:02 | 100mi

---

## Race Overview
- 100+ miles from London to Battle Abbey
- 30-hour cut-off
- Follows the historic route of King Harold's battle-march
- Finish line under the Gatehouse of Battle Abbey, on the edge of the battlefield of Hastings

## Historical Significance
- 1066: King Harold of England, fresh from defeating Vikings in York, marched back to London
- Then marched south towards Hastings upon hearing of invading William of Normandy
- A long distance path called "Harold's Way" marks the battle-march route from Westminster Abbey to Battle Abbey

## Race Character
- Register for "battle" in London, along the banks of the Thames
- Harold's army had 3 days for the march
- Runners have just 30 hours to replicate it
- English Heritage permitted the finish under Battle Abbey Gatehouse
- 100-mile qualifier for races requiring such qualification

## Course Details
- Current route is 102 miles (slightly shorter than 2019 route with some faster ground)
- Point-to-point from London to Battle

## Race Organisation
- Organised by Beyond Marathon Ltd and Race Drone Events Tracking Ltd
- Same organisers run: King Offa's Dyke Race, Escape From Meriden, Deadwater, Convergence, Dusk til Dawn, Millennium Way Ultra, Gritstone Grind, Wychavon Way, 12 Labours of Hercules

## Context for Stephen

- Replicated Harold's route in under 27 hours
