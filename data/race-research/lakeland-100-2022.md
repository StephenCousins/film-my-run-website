# Montane Lakeland 100 2022 - Research

**Stephen's Race:** 29/07/2022 | 34:36:56 | 105mi

---

## Race Overview
- Actually 105 miles with over 6000 metres of ascent
- Starts and finishes in Coniston
- Loop of the Lakes with almost non-stop ascent and descent
- Normal failure rate: 40-50% over the 100 mile course
- 2022: 1,865 starters across both 50 and 100 events

## 2022 Weather Conditions
- Extremely wet, muddy and muggy conditions
- "The heavens opened and the Lake District showed just how wet it can really get"
- Relentless wet weather made the race even more challenging
- Climb from Fusedale to Haweswater: rain and strong, cold headwind

## 2022 Race Results

### Lakeland 100
- **Winner:** Andy Berry - 20:03:11
- Overtook long-time leader Jarlath McKenna in final 6km from Tiberthwaite checkpoint
- Berry's first 100-mile race
- Earlier that year he broke Kim Collison's Lakeland 24-hour summits record
- McKenna (2nd) - also his first 100-miler

### Lakeland 50
- **Men:** Neil MacNicol - 8:03:58 (twice placed second in Lakeland 100 previously)
- **Women:** Katie Kaars Sijpesteijn - 8:02:19 (NEW WOMEN'S RECORD despite conditions)

## Course Character
- Ever-changing vistas through the Lake District
- Darkness and tricky navigation add to challenge
- Brutal combination of climb, descent, and rugged terrain

## Context for Stephen
- 34:36:56 finish in truly awful conditions
- Over 34 hours through the wet Lake District
- Significantly longer than the winner's time, but finished in conditions that broke many
