# Val d'Aran by UTMB 100 Mile 2021 - Research

**Stephen's Race:** 09/07/2021 | 46:50:32 | 100mi

---

## Race Overview
- 162.90 km with 10,690 metres of elevation gain
- Comparable profile to UTMB itself: ~100 miles, 10,000m climbing
- Tops out at over 2,500m elevation
- Located in the Spanish Pyrenees
- 941 finishers in 2021

## COVID Context
- 2020 race was cancelled due to pandemic
- Runners deferred to 2021
- Getting to Spain wasn't straightforward or cheap: COVID tests, forms, last-minute cancelled flights

## Race Start Chaos
- Started at 6pm
- Congested start with so many runners on single track
- About 4km in, runners came to a COMPLETE STANDSTILL for around 20 minutes
- 970 runners filtering through a particularly narrow section

## Difficulty Level
- Described as "without question the hardest race I have ever done" by multiple runners
- Severity of terrain + altitude + distance + steepness of climbs
- "The edge as my toughest challenge yet"
- Runners reported legs being shot on descents, unable to even jog
- Got hotter and hotter, becoming "more and more miserable"

## Course Quality
- Very well marked
- Aid stations were well-stocked

## Special UTMB Qualification
- For one year only, completers were given automatic entry to the full UTMB 100 in Chamonix
- No ballot process required

## Context for Stephen
- 46:50:32 finish time - nearly 47 hours
- This earned automatic UTMB 100 qualification
- You went on to complete UTMB 100 in 2023
