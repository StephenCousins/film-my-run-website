# UTMB OCC 2024 - Research

**Stephen's Race:** 29/08/2024 | 11:39:18 | 34mi (57km)

---

## Race Overview
- OCC = Orsières - Champex-Lac - Chamonix
- 57km with 3,500m (11,500 feet) of elevation gain
- Three big climbs
- ~1,800 runners from 90 different countries
- Often called "The Little Swiss Sister of UTMB"
- Started 8:15am local time, Thursday August 29

## 2024 Course
- Matched the 2022 track (longer than 2023)
- 2023 had a shorter course due to rockslide closure
- Wave start system: 3 waves with 15 minutes between each

## 2024 Weather Conditions
- Hot-weather kit requirements activated
- Temperatures climbing to 21°C (70°F) range
- Winner Miao Yao: "the heat was a challenge today and hydration was very important"
- Eli Hemming: "on the last climb to La Flégère I was very much overheated and the walls started closing in"

## 2024 Results

### Men
- **Winner:** Eli Hemming (USA) - 5:11:48
- First American male to win OCC
- Switched from triathlon to trail running just 3 years ago
- Had trouble on final climb, briefly stopped near mile 31, recovered on downhill
- 2nd: Puppi - 5:14:46
- 3rd: Martínez - 5:17:56

### Women
- **Winner:** Miao Yao (China) - 5:54:03 (new best time)
- Won CCC in 2018, third in OCC 2023
- Made move on big climb from Trient to Col de Balme (highest point)
- "Blew the race apart" on that climb, never threatened after
- 2nd: Judith Wyder (Switzerland)
- 3rd: Clémentine Geoffray (France)

## Course Highlights
- "Absolutely stunning views throughout"
- Col de Balme: highest point on course
- Finish through Chamonix: "magical" - strangers cheering, high fives
- "That last kilometer through town was pretty magical"

## Context for Stephen
