# Tenerife Bluetrail 2019 - Research

**Stephen's Race:** 07/06/2019 | 22:02:42 | 62mi (102km)

---

## Race Overview
- Part of the Fred. Olson Tenerife Blue Trail weekend
- 102km main event, started Friday 23:30
- Full crossing of the island: sea in the south → Teide (highest point) → north
- Unique course: begins at sea level and ascends 3,500 metres
- Part goes through Teide National Park
- Highest Spanish and second-highest European race

## 2019 Weather Conditions
- In contrast to 2018: warmer and less windy for the night journey to Teide
- Only later in the afternoon on Saturday did low cloud arrive with intermittent rain

## 2019 Results

### Men
- **Winner:** Yeray Duran - 11:48:09
- Despite early charge by Sange Sherpa, Duran controlled the race
- Bided his time early evening, had firm grip by dawn
- Crossed Teide and dropped down to north for comfortable win

### Women
- **Winner:** Leire Martinez Herrera - 14:36:42
- Ran strong and controlled race
- 2nd: Marta Muixi Casaldaliga - 14:49
- 3rd: Irene Guembe Ibanez - 15:23:10
- Victory by Leire "never in question" in the second half

## Course Character
- Unlike any other European race
- Night start, climbing through darkness toward Teide
- 3,500m total elevation gain
- Volcanic landscape of Teide National Park
- Finish in the north of the island

## Context for Stephen
- 22:02:42 finish - just over 22 hours
- Night start, climbing to Teide in darkness
- Dealt with the unique challenge of starting at sea level and reaching 3,500m
